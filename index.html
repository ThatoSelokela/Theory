Theory Overview

My theory suggests that life originated through a process driven by quantum resonance and thermodynamic self-organization.

Core Idea

The theory actually drives the Miller-Urey experiment

Components

  • Quantum Resonance: This is about how tiny particles, like atoms and molecules, can "resonate" or vibrate at certain frequencies. Think of it like how a guitar string vibrates when you pluck it, creating a sound. In the quantum world, particles can vibrate or resonate in special ways that affect how they interact with each other.
  • Thermodynamic Self-Organization: This refers to how things naturally organize themselves into more complex structures due to the flow of energy. For example, when you pour cream into coffee, the cream spreads out and mixes by itself. Similarly, in nature, energy can cause simple things to arrange themselves into more organized patterns or systems without any outside help.
  • Emergence of Proto-Life: This term talks about how the first simple forms of life might have appeared on Earth. It refers to the process where non-living chemicals started to come together in ways that led to the earliest forms of life, like very simple cells or molecules that could make copies of themselves.
